## Techniques & Methods
- **AI as rehearsal, not substitute** — Key mental model: when AI replaces interpersonal discomfort (e.g., giving feedback, navigating conflict), it erodes social trust. When it scales skills practiced in person (reflective summaries, practice scenarios), it strengthens performance. Framework: build human capacity locally first → use AI to extend and reinforce at scale.
- **WEF 2025 skills framework** — Prioritize: analytical thinking, creativity, resilience, leadership. Assume these will NOT be automated away in how people actually work together, even as AI handles more individual tasks.

## Industry Trends
- The central tension in enterprise AI adoption: AI is most dangerous when it substitutes for interpersonal discomfort; most valuable when it amplifies skills already built in person.
- "Trust isn't restored through intention — it's earned through participation." (Chappy Asel, AI Collective Co-Founder)
- AI adoption timescales are organizational (months), not societal (centuries) — governance and measurement decisions are more time-sensitive than historical tech transitions.
- The $2 billion/day incivility cost (SHRM) and Gen Z as 30% of workforce by the late 2020s are structural pressures creating demand for in-person capacity building at scale.
