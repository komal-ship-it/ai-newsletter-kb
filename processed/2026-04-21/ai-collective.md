## Key Announcements
- **AI has a speed problem in both directions** — Lauren Slyman: most AI deployments never reach production; RAND Corporation finds AI/ML projects fail at 2x the rate of non-AI tech projects.

## Tools & Products
- None today.

## Techniques & Methods
- **Operational ROI Framework** — Define enterprise AI ROI as: latency reduction, error prevention, improved decision cycles, system resilience. Avoid speed-only metrics that set up retrenchment.
- **Organizational Readiness Before AI** — Data quality, governance, workflows, and system clarity are prerequisites, not afterthoughts. Skipping them causes cascading failures.
- **Slow the Narrative** — Actively prevent internal expectations from outrunning system capability. Hype misalignment causes projects to fail even when the technology works.

## Research Papers
- **BCG GPT-4 Study** — GPT-4 users performed 40% better on creative product innovation but 23% worse on solving business problems. Specialization matters.
- **GitHub Copilot Benchmark** — Tasks completed 55.8% faster, but downstream work (debugging, integration, maintenance) not counted. Gains may be partially offset.
- **Anthropic Internal Research** — AI speeds up tasks by up to 80%; but one AI-assisted group scored 17% lower than no-AI group. Largest skill gap: debugging knowledge atrophy.

## Industry Trends
- Enterprise AI failure rate double that of non-AI projects (RAND).
- VC and enterprise adoption timelines fundamentally misaligned: VC expects rapid scaling, enterprise runs on compliance, procurement, and risk aversion.
- AI debugging skill atrophy emerging as a measurable side effect of AI-assisted coding.
