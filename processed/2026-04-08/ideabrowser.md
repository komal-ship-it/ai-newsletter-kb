## Techniques & Methods
- **The Third-Place Test (Starbucks framework)** — Diagnose whether you're selling a transaction or an environment: "If a competitor built your top feature at half the price, would your customers switch?" If yes, you're in a transaction business. Identify what customers can't get elsewhere — that's your environment. Protect it like a product.
- **VA hiring documentation as operations documentation** — "Hiring a VA starts with a question founders never answer out loud: what do you do all day? Describe it clearly enough for a stranger, and that document runs your business long after the VA quits." — Process documentation as a forcing function.
- **Matching algorithm cold-start strategy** — Start with manual vetting (30-50 VAs across key categories), launch to 10 businesses, measure time-to-hire vs. Upwork experience. Algorithm begins as basic scoring, sharpens as completed project data accumulates. Rehire rate = matching works; continued browsing = recalibrate vetting criteria.
- **Niche discovery in bureaucratic friction** — Federal meal reimbursement for home daycare: money is pre-approved, but complexity of filing kills uptake. Pattern: find markets where money is left unclaimed due to process friction, not eligibility.

## Industry Trends
- **VA marketplace fatigue** — Upwork's proposal-flood problem (50 identical proposals per job) creates genuine demand for pre-vetting. Time-to-hire friction is the real pain, not finding candidates.
- **In-home daycare as a collapsing market** — 97,000 licensed home daycares shut between 2005–2017. Federal reimbursement programs exist but are systematically unclaimed due to filing complexity. App-based simplification = strong retention moat ($4–8K/year recaptured).
- **Starbucks "third place" lesson** — Products that create environments (rituals, places to belong) have stronger retention than feature-based products. Applies to SaaS: what ritual does your product enable that no competitor can copy?
