## Techniques & Methods
- **Agents as Digital Employees framework** — Reframe AI agents from "tools" to "digital employees": give them onboarding, scoped access, accountability, and a manager. Move controls to the execution/runtime layer, not just the access layer. Principle: when oversight costs more thinking than the work an agent replaces, redesign the trust model.
- **Policy-based runtime restrictions** — Enforce agent behavior restrictions at runtime via policy frameworks rather than trusting agents to self-govern. Protects against credential misuse and indirect prompt injection.
- **Distribution-first growth** — AI Collective panel insight: build channel partnerships and distribution infrastructure while you still have bandwidth (not after the first growth stall). Founder-led sales is a ceiling, not a foundation.

## Research Papers
- **Multilingual Agent Benchmarks — "Functional Alignment Gap" (Lilt)** — Most agent benchmarks are English-centric. Machine-translated tasks often break the underlying logic of an agent's tools. Evaluation frameworks must account for cultural nuances and linguistic complexity, not just literal translation. | https://lilt.com/blog/multilingual-agent-benchmarks-beyond-translation
