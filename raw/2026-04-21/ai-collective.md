By Lauren Slyman. A dinner conversation landed on: AI is moving too quickly and too slowly at the same time.

THE ECOSYSTEM FEELS UNBALANCED
Most AI deployments still do not reach production. RAND Corporation estimates AI/ML projects fail at roughly 2x the rate of non-AI tech projects.

WHERE VENTURE LOGIC BREAKS IN ENTERPRISE REALITY
VC mindset (rapid scaling, high tolerance for failure) conflicts with how enterprises operate. Heavy investment without clear ROI poses risk of retrenchment similar to the dot-com era.

ROI NEEDS AN ENTERPRISE DEFINITION
Enterprises do not get ten shots to justify one win. Chasing hype metrics jeopardizes compounding value from patient process work.

THREE PRACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
1. Define ROI operationally — latency reduction, error prevention, improved decision cycles, system resilience.
2. Invest in organizational readiness — data quality, governance, workflows, system clarity are not optional.
3. Slow the narrative — prevent internal expectations from outrunning what the system can safely support.

EVIDENCE: TASK GAINS, SYSTEM-LEVEL TRADEOFFS
- BCG: GPT-4 users performed 40% better on creative product innovation, but 23% worse on solving business problems.
- GitHub Copilot: tasks completed 55.8% faster, but this ignores downstream work (debugging, integration, maintenance).
- Anthropic research: AI can speed up tasks by up to 80%, but one AI group scored 17% lower than those who coded without AI. Largest gap was around debugging knowledge.

CLOSING THOUGHTS
AI is simultaneously overhyped and incredibly useful. The question is how to balance speed with durability aligned to an organization's constraints.
