### 🧑‍💼 AI Copilot for Managers

_A Few Good Managers_

**The Problem:** There are 83 million managers in the United States alone. Most of them are winging it. The typical first-time manager gets promoted because they were great at their individual job - which has absolutely nothing to do with running a team. And yet companies hand them direct reports, a calendar full of 1:1s, and a performance review template and say "good luck". The result? Disengaged teams, missed reviews, vague feedback, and a lot of very stressed people pretending they know what they're doing. The global cost of poor management is estimated at over $8.8 trillion in lost productivity every year. That's not a people problem. That's a tooling problem. Here's what we're thinking.

**The Solution:**

💡 **The Idea:** An AI-powered copilot for people managers to help them to become great managers

🛠️ **Product:**

* Connect your calendar and the AI automatically joins every 1:1, captures the conversation, logs action items, and sends you a pre-meeting brief before each session - surfacing what was discussed last time, open items, and suggested talking points based on how each direct report is trending.

* After each meeting, the AI analyses patterns across your entire team - flagging things like "you've spoken 80% of the time in your last 4 meetings with Sarah" or "you haven't given James positive feedback in 6 weeks."

* At review season, the copilot auto-drafts a full performance review for each team member from the year's worth of 1:1 transcripts, sentiment data, and logged feedback - formatted for whatever HR system the company uses.

🧑‍💻 **Prototyping:** Try the [demo](https://overseen-286359934050.us-west1.run.app) | Get the [demo prompt](https://www.notion.so/Demo-Prompt-Overseen-33b8d2b9402b808aaf16fc78cc52aee8?pvs=21)

💼 **Business Model:** $25-50/month per manager self-serve; $10-30k/year enterprise deals sold through HR teams.

⭐ **End Goal:** Get acquired by Workday, BambooHR, Microsoft (Teams), or a talent management platform like Lattice or Culture Amp looking to own the manager layer. Target exit 8-12x ARR.

### 🎨 How to Use Google Stitch Like a Pro

**The Context:** Google Stitch just became one of the most powerful free tools available to founders - it turns plain English into high-fidelity UI designs in minutes. The problem? Most people type one vague sentence and wonder why the output looks generic. Here's what to do instead.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Go to stitch.withgoogle.com and sign in with your Google account. You're on the canvas in under a minute.

2. Before you prompt, define two things: who is using this, and how do you want them to feel? "A busy founder who needs to feel in control" produces a completely different design to "a teenager who wants to feel like a pro trader."

3. Hit the '+' button and use the prompt enhancer. It translates vague words like "modern" or "sporty" into specific design language Stitch actually understands - think "architectural limestone" or "high-end stationery." That specificity is the difference between generic and intentional.

4. Tell Stitch your colour hierarchy explicitly: Neutral for the canvas, Primary for key UI elements, Tertiary for your loudest accents - used sparingly. Say it in the prompt and the output looks structured, not chaotic.
