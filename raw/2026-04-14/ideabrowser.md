## 📌 IDEA OF THE DAY: Brand consistency scoring mechanism for video agencies
April 14, 2026

Every agency has an unwritten list of which clients get frame-by-frame review and which ones ship on faith. The ranking is based on who complained last.

**Brandgrade** takes a client-approved reference video and scores new deliverables against it. An editor uploads a cut and the system analyzes color grading, typography, transitions, and pacing, returning a score with a breakdown of what deviates and by how much.

- Profiles save per client so every future cut scores against the same baseline
- Start with 5 agencies across different verticals — upload reference videos for top clients
- Ship objective metrics first (color grading, typography); let pacing/transition scoring mature through real client work

Pricing: $99-$299/month. At 1,000 agencies averaging $200/month, annual revenue crosses $2.4M.

## 📚 FOUNDER PLAYBOOK: The market competitors won't touch is the one worth taking

Sam Walton opened the first Walmart in Rogers, Arkansas because Sears and Kmart had a hard rule: no stores in towns under 50,000. That left thousands of communities with zero discount retail.

Walton built 1,928 stores below that line. Lower rent, lower labor costs, no incumbent to compete with on price. $55B in revenue by 1992.

He walked Kmart stores with a notepad — measured aisle widths, counted checkout lines, wrote down prices. The company building in towns Kmart wouldn't enter was taking notes inside the stores Kmart wouldn't leave.

**The Threshold Map — Steal This:**
1. Pick the biggest player in any market
2. Find their floor: minimum deal size, customer size, market they'll enter
3. Count what's below each line — that count is the beachhead
