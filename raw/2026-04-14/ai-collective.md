The Byte: "The Default State" by Oana Ruxandra

In "The Default State," Oana Ruxandra argues that today's shift toward AI-enabled creation is less a sudden "AI disruption" than a return to a much older human pattern: most people making, building, and trading across many skills rather than spending decades inside narrow roles.

---

For most of human history, before industrialization reorganized the world around specialization, roughly 80-90% of people worked across farming, crafting, trading, and repair. In 1550 England, approximately 75% of laborers were classified as versatile. By 1850, that fell to 55%. By the mid-20th century, roughly 70% of US workers were inside large firms doing specialized tasks.

"The idea that a person would spend 40 years doing one narrow thing inside one institution is not the human condition. It is a specific economic arrangement, roughly 150 years old, that peaked in living memory. And it is ending."

The evidence:
- [Unemployment for college graduates ages 22 to 27 recently hit 5.6%](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/24/business/economy/college-graduates-job-market-hiring.html) — worst since pandemic depths
- [Independent music revenue grew from $692M in 2015 to $1.58B by 2018](https://routenote.com/blog/independent-music-is-the-fastest-growing-part-of-the-industry/), now accounting for half of Spotify's $11B in music payouts
- Annual US small business applications held above 5M for 5 consecutive years — permanent reset from pre-pandemic ceiling of 3.5M
- LinkedIn members adding 'founder' to profiles jumped 60% year over year, nearly tripled since 2022

## Music As The First Undoing

From Warner Music Group, the gatekeepers were not protecting quality. They were protecting a distribution model. When the barriers fell, artists who had been told they were not artists started making their work.

## The Same Pattern, One Industry Later

Starting a company now looks like music production looked in 2015. Engineering, design, distribution — things that once required institutional backing — are now accessible to anyone with a clear problem and determination. The desire was never the constraint. The access was.

1 in 3 US adults plans to start a business or side hustle in 2026, a 94% increase over the prior year. Among those already planning to launch, 65% say they will use AI to help do it.

## Which World Are You Building For?

AI is already being grafted onto old models, old founder archetypes, old assumptions about who is building and why. The technology moves first, and the understanding of the person catches up later.

This time, when it catches up, it will catch up to something that runs deeper than any recent consumer behavior or trend. Something suppressed for 150 years is now decompressing all at once.
