🏊 Lead Gen for Pool Companies — Making a Splash

The Problem: There are over 10 million residential swimming pools in the USA with between 500K and 1M new pools built each year. Pool builders are still finding customers the old-fashioned way - buying shared leads from HomeAdvisor, running Facebook ads, or praying for referrals. Meanwhile, there are millions of mid-market homes sitting on perfect lots with big empty backyards.

The Solution:
💡 The Idea: A lead generation system that uses AI to identify houses with empty backyards and renders a pool in their backyard from a partner provider.

🛠️ Product:
- The system scans satellite imagery for mid-market homes ($500K-$1.2M) without pools, filtering by lot size, sun exposure, and recent ownership changes.
- For each prospect, AI renders a luxury pool into their actual backyard, calculates the build cost and home value lift for their specific zip code, and generates a cinematic video walkthrough.
- A personalised postcard with the before/after and a QR code is printed and mailed automatically, while the system simultaneously hits the homeowner with retargeting ads.

🧑‍💻 Prototyping: [Try the demo](https://poolbuoy-ai-344780963302.us-west1.run.app/app)

💼 Business Model: Charge $50-100 per qualified lead on a pay-per-lead model. Premium tier at $3-5k/mo includes the video rendering and retargeting automation.

⭐ End Goal: Acquired by a home services marketplace (Angi, HomeAdvisor), a CRM player (ServiceTitan, Jobber), or a proptech platform.

📧 How to Write Cold Emails That Actually Get Replies

1. Kill the subject line first — write something specific enough that only one person could have received it.
2. Keep the whole email to five lines — hook, why it matters to them, your ask, a low-friction CTA, sign off.
3. Follow up without apology — most replies come from follow-ups not the first email.
4. Track and iterate — send 100 emails, track what gets replies, kill what doesn't.
