## IDEA OF THE DAY
**Apprenticeship matching for trade school graduates** — Guildpost
April 5, 2026

Trade school enrollment is growing while the skilled labor shortage worsens. The path between school and work has outdated infrastructure. Graduates check bulletins, ask instructors, and scroll job sites that bury trade postings. Contractors call the same contacts. A class of students and a shop floor short on welders are 10 miles apart with no way to find each other.

On Guildpost, a graduate uploads certifications, tool experience, and shift preferences. A contractor posts an apprenticeship with the details: union/non-union, residential/commercial, travel radius, and progression timeline. The matching runs on what the person can do, not what keywords they typed. The profile built on graduation day carries forward through journeyman certification and beyond.

Trade schools are the distribution partner because placement rates drive enrollment numbers. Start with three to five schools in high-demand trades like HVAC and electrical. The constraint is employer density. Trades hiring is local. Prove the match works in two metros where both sides show up before expanding geography.

Contractors pay $150 to $500/month for candidate pool access plus $50 to $200 for premium listings. Graduates use the platform free. Distribution compounds through trade school partnerships.
