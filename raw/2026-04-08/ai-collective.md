🎵 ElevenMusic: Turn a Text Prompt into a Full Song, Right on Your Phone
ElevenLabs launched ElevenMusic on April 1, a free iOS app that generates songs from natural language prompts. Describe a genre, set a mood, choose whether you want lyrics, and the app builds a full track in seconds. The free tier covers seven songs a day and a $9.99/month Pro option. ElevenMusic runs on a single prompt input: describe genre, writing style, and mood. Songs can be remixed from other users' public tracks, and the app includes curated live stations and daily mixes organized by mood: Focus, Energy, Relax, Late Night, Cosmic, and Chill. ElevenLabs raised a $500M Series C at an $11B valuation in February 2026.

🎙️ Google AI Edge Eloquent: Dictation That Runs Entirely on Your Phone
Google quietly pushed an iOS app called AI Edge Eloquent to the App Store on April 6. Its defining feature: it doesn't send your audio to the cloud. The app downloads Google's Gemma speech recognition model directly to your phone, transcribes everything locally, and requires no internet connection after the initial setup. It's free, with no usage caps. After a one-time download of the on-device Gemma ASR model, all transcription runs locally. When you pause recording, the app automatically removes filler words and copies clean prose to your clipboard. The underlying stack — LiteRT-LM — supports the same Gemma model family on Android, iOS, desktop, and Raspberry Pi.

🫀 Vox: A Daily 5-Second Voice Recording That Screens for Heart Failure
Heart failure causes over a million US hospitalizations each year. Noah Labs built Vox to change that detection window. The system analyzes a 5-second daily voice recording to identify acoustic changes linked to cardiac congestion, sometimes 21 days before hospitalization becomes necessary. In April, the FDA granted Vox Breakthrough Device designation, fast-tracking US regulatory review. Vox detects changes in vocal fold acoustics linked to pulmonary congestion and fluid overload. The model was trained on more than 3 million voice samples and validated across five sites including the Mayo Clinic and UCSF.
