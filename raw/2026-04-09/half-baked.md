### IDEA: Bloomberg Terminal for Science Breakthroughs

"Lab-solutely essential"

The Problem: AI is compressing decades of drug discovery and materials science into years, and the 4 million papers published annually is about to go vertical. Companies need a tool to keep up with all this change. Inspired by reading The Infinity Machine (biography of Demis Hassabis).

The Solution:
- The Idea: A Bloomberg-style scientific intelligence platform that monitors every major journal globally and surfaces cross-field breakthroughs for organisations that compete on scientific knowledge.
- Product: A pharma company, hedge fund, or consulting firm connects their focus areas and the platform maps the entire global research landscape against their strategic priorities 24/7. Every morning, decision-makers get a tailored intelligence briefing - their sector's top developments plus a "Cross-Field Spark" flagging breakthroughs from adjacent disciplines. Deep-dive mode lets teams query the entire scientific literature like a conversation.
- Business Model: $50k-$500k/year enterprise contracts with pharma companies, hedge funds, and consulting firms.
- End Goal: Get acquired by Bloomberg, Elsevier, Springer Nature, or a major pharma like Pfizer or AstraZeneca. Target exit $500M-$2B at 8-12x ARR.

### How to Build Your First Agent with Claude

Claude's just released "Managed Agents" and it's surprisingly easy to spin up your first AI agent. Steps:
1. Head to Claude Console and click into Quickstart under Managed Agents.
2. Define what you want your agent to do, or pick one of the pre-built templates.
3. Work with Claude directly inside the Console to refine your agent's behaviour, test it against real inputs, and deploy when you're happy.
