## 🖥️ Cross-Device Testing Tool — "No device left behind"

**The Problem:** Over 24,000 distinct Android devices are in active use today, yet dev teams test on roughly 10. Your landing page might look perfect on your MacBook but be completely broken on the Samsung Galaxy A15 that 40 million people actually use — ruining UX and losing sales.

**The Solution (PixelProof):**

- Connect your domain in 60 seconds — no code required. Immediately runs pages through 5,000+ virtual device, browser, OS, and resolution combinations.
- AI vision models scan every render around the clock, flag broken layouts, hidden CTAs, and illegible text, ranking issues by real-world market share of that device.
- Slack alert on breakage: "🚨 Your checkout page just broke on Android Chrome (affects 14% of your traffic) — here's the fix."

## 🧠 How to Set Up Your Second Brain in 5 Minutes

**The Context:** The LLM Wiki pattern, popularised by Andrej Karpathy, has AI write and maintain your knowledge base. Your job: add sources and ask good questions. Claude's job: everything else.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Download Obsidian (free) and create a vault — a folder of markdown files. Install Claude Code and point it at that folder.
2. Set up three things inside your vault: `/raw-sources/` for content you want to add, `/wiki/` where Claude builds and maintains all your knowledge pages, and a `CLAUDE.md` file that tells Claude how your wiki is structured.
3. Drop a file into `/raw-sources/`, tell Claude to process it, and watch it update 10–15 wiki pages — summaries, concept pages, cross-references, all filed automatically.
4. Once a week, run a lint pass asking Claude to find contradictions, orphan pages, and outdated claims.

Full tutorial: https://x.com/defileo/status/2042241063612502162
