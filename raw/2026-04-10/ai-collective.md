## 🌍 The 80% Who Are Quietly Refusing AI

A new Fortune investigation breaks down what's actually happening inside the enterprise. 54% of workers bypassed their company's AI tools in the past 30 days to finish tasks manually. Another 33% haven't touched AI at all.

Key data points:
- 61% of executives trust AI for complex decisions. Only 9% of workers agree.
- 88% of executives believe their teams have adequate tools. Only 21% of workers say the same.
- Workers are losing 51 working days a year to technology friction, up 42% from 2025.
- WalkMe's CEO: fewer than 10% of employees are doing meaningful AI work today.

Source: https://fortune.com/2026/04/09/ai-backlash-quiet-quitting-fobo-obsolete-white-collar-rebellion/

## 🌍 Your Next Coworker Is an Agent. Treat It Like One.

Richard Beck of QA (published on ISACA) argues that organizations succeeding with agentic AI are those who stopped thinking about agents as clever tools and started treating them as digital employees — with onboarding, access scopes, accountability, and a manager.

Key security problems:
- Agents operate with real user credentials, so traditional endpoint detection and DLP systems read their activity as legitimate behavior.
- Indirect prompt injection: attackers hide instructions inside an email or document, and the agent executes them without knowing it crossed a line.
- Community skill registries add a supply chain surface most teams have never threat-modeled.

The builders getting this right are moving controls down to the execution layer — policy-based frameworks that enforce restrictions at runtime instead of trusting the agent to behave.

Key principle: when oversight costs more thinking than the work an agent replaces, the trust model is wrong and it's time to redesign it.

Source: https://www.isaca.org/resources/news-and-trends/isaca-now-blog/2026/agentic-ai-evolution-and-the-security-claw
