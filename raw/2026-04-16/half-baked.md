SOUR STRIPS FOR LIFTERS

The Problem: DTC is back baby. Grüns just got acquired for >$1B, Huel too, and investors are warming up to consumer brands again - especially ones that take an existing behaviour and make it cleaner. A super viral tweet (>12M views) broke down the science of why serious weight lifters eat Sour Patch Kids or Haribo in between sets. The science checks out - fast carbs enter your bloodstream immediately, working muscles pull that glucose straight in, and you bypass the usual metabolic bottlenecks to stay fuelled mid-session. The problem? Haribo and Sour Patch Kids are loaded with artificial colours, flavours, and ingredients your body has zero use for.

The Solution:
- The Idea: A premium, healthy sour strip brand that delivers the same glycemic response as sweets but with clean-label ingredients and precise macros.
- Product: The core formula swaps Haribo's glucose syrup and gelatine for dextrose and rice syrup (identical glycemic index, cleaner label) plus fruit pectin instead of gelatine - making it vegan, same chew texture. Every 50g single-serve pouch delivers exactly 30g of fast-digesting carbs. Colour comes from fruit and vegetable extracts, not Red 40.
- Business Model: DTC at $2.50-3.00 per 50g pouch, subscription boxes of 20 pouches at $50/month. Wholesale to supplement retailers and gym front desks at 40% margin.
- End Goal: Get acquired by a sports nutrition incumbent like Glanbia (Optimum Nutrition), MyProtein or a company like Unilever. Target exit 4-6x revenue once the brand owns the category.

HOW TO CREATE A MORNING BRIEF USING NOTION AGENTS

Notion's Custom Agents are fully autonomous AI teammates that run on a schedule with zero prompting. The Morning Brief agent scans your workspace, calendar, mail, and Slack every morning and delivers a single page telling you exactly what needs your attention.

Step-by-step:
1. Go to Notion's Agent Marketplace, install the Morning Brief template, and hit Edit to configure it. The logic is pre-built - you're just pointing it at your tools.
2. Connect your sources - Notion databases, Google Calendar, Notion Mail, Slack - and tell it in plain English what to prioritise.
3. Under Triggers, set it to Schedule, pick your time (7am works well), hit Activate, and you're done. Fresh brief, every morning, automatically.
