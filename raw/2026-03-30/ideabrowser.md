## IDEA OF THE DAY
**Government contract application platform for small businesses** — Tendly
March 30, 2026

Government contracting is a trillion-dollar market. Contracts are posted. Deadlines are listed. Requirements are published. All public, all open. But finding the right contract and filing before the window closes takes a team that small businesses don't have. The contracts go to whoever finds them first and files correctly. Right now, that's the company with the biggest back office.

Tendly matches small businesses to government contracts they qualify for. Enter your industry, location, and certifications once. The platform scans live postings, filters by fit, and alerts you before the deadline hits. Each bid sharpens the match.

Start with a scraper that pulls from major government contract databases and matches listings against a business profile. Build deadline alerts and document checklists before the dashboard gets any attention. The honest challenge is SAM.gov: it's free, it has the same data, and small business owners have tried it and walked away. Tendly wins by making the same public data usable on a small business timeline.

Tendly charges $29/month for basic alerts and $79/month for advanced filtering and profile matching. A single $50K contract won covers seven years of subscription fees.

## FOUNDER PLAYBOOK
**Industry calluses close deals that pitch decks can't**

Tracy Young spent four years as a construction engineer hauling 3,000-page blueprint binders across job sites. When Apple launched the iPad in 2010, she saw the answer before the tech industry noticed the question. PlanGrid put blueprints on tablets. Zero salespeople to $5M ARR. Young and her co-founders showed up at job sites and demoed at prospects' doors. $200 deals got an email with a demo link. Six-figure deals got three co-founders at the door. Autodesk paid $875M in 2018.
